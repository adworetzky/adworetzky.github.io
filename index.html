The Race Is Over!

This is it, folks. You came, you rolled, you conquered. The marble race has officially come to an end. Stage 5 was a mess, let me tell you.

Stage 5 gave advantage to deformed marbles. While Andie Thomas and Jeremy Alvarez knew it was their time to shine, you could hear Bethany Powell curse under her breath. With physics broken, it was like the wild west. But the racers knew this was for all the “marbles.” Josephine threw lettuce at Ruben. Kian came out brandishing a cleaver. Stefan stole 3 identities mid race. It was a mess (it looked a little like this.) But amidst the turmoil, 3 racers emerged victorious for the stage.

Our top three finishers for stage 5 were 1.Andie Thomas, 2.Genevieve Henderson, and 3.Robyn Parker.

Our final first-place finish for the 2020 Marble Madness Competition goes to Franklin Mason (Team Lee Banse.) He would like to thank his sponsors Lee Banse, Pablo Escobar (for single-handedly importing all the hippos in Colombia), and Heinz. He plans to celebrate by rubbing it in Kian James’ face while live-streaming it to his base of 47,000 fans.

Second place goes to Rory Walsh (Team Heather Mahendran.) Rory would like to thank her sponsor, Heather, and would also like to yell at Halliburton for pulling their sponsorship of her after Stage 3. When approached for comment Halliburton stated that Rory was too volatile for “even them.” Rory’s celebration will be later tonight when she lights fireworks in an enclosed trailer and “lets it play out.”

Our biggest surprise of this competition was perhaps our last-place finisher, Aubry Reynolds (Team Tim Grose.) He planned, he executed, and he lost terribly. Handing Aubry the last place trophy was kind of like pinning a participation medal on someone's chest, but involving a grown man… while crying.
