As we all know, sports are canceled for the forseeable future. So why not keep the spirit of competition alive with good-old-fashion gravity (with a digital twist). That's right. You heard me. Balls. Balls and Inclines.

Let's get weird.

Ok, here's how it works. I have 32 digital marbles, each of them with a unique color, personality, voter history, favorite sushi, and other things that make them hella different. I've given them each a personality that you can vibe with so you don't feel like you're picking numbers our of a hat. Over the next 4 days, I will virtually race them all together in stages using a simulated particle physics algorithm projected through weird 3D shapes that I come up with because the 'rona be REAL and I've got time(note: because this is a physic simulation based on the math, there isn't an actual "race" per se to watch. Just use your imagination and trust the math). The order in which the marbles finish get assigned a point value going from 32 to 1 and they race over 5 stages.

I know what your thinking... Isn't this basically flipping a coin to see which magic ball of glass gets the trophy? No dice, muchacho. Each "marble" is given a random weight between 2 and 5 grams as well as randomized spatial deformities (rated on scale of 1-10, 1= little deformity 10= dude, they've seen some shit) which make it behave uniquely when fed into the magic physics machine (it's just the unity engine without any graphics, what did you expect? I'm not made of money). But that means that truly some marbles will be better suited than others for racing. To compound that, the stages will give advantage or disadvantage to marbles depending on their attributes. Think: DnD meets marble racing (weight is like a dex modifier and deformity is like have a low acrobatics).

Now, your job is to guess based on weight and deformity what the magic math machine will deem worthy of the winning. Or just pick based on their names like a schmuck, who gives a shit. I mean, I'm the one doing the math here, you're job is easy.
